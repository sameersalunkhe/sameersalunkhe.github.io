About Me

Hello! I'm Sameer, an astrophysicist focused on radio astronomy and the study of galaxy clusters. I received my Ph.D. in Physics from Savitribai Phule Pune University with a thesis titled "Unravelling the Unseen Matter in the Universe through Radio Emission from Low Mass Galaxy Clusters" under the supervision of Dr. Surajit Paul.

Currently working as a Post Doctoral Fellow at the National Centre for Radio Astrophysics - Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (NCRA-TIFR) in Pune, India. My research involves using radio observations to study diffuse emission in galaxy clusters, which provides insights into cosmic magnetism and particle acceleration processes in the universe.

I have extensive experience with radio astronomical techniques and data analysis using tools like CASA, AIPS, SPAM pipeline, and WSCLEAN. I'm passionate about uncovering the mysteries of the universe's large-scale structures through observation and analysis.

Research Interests

Radio Astronomy

Using radio telescopes like uGMRT and JVLA to study cosmic phenomena. Specializing in observational techniques for detecting faint diffuse radio emission from galaxy clusters and large-scale structures.

Galaxy Clusters & Intracluster Medium

Investigating the properties and evolution of galaxy clusters, with particular focus on low-mass clusters. Studying diffuse radio features like halos, relics, and mini-halos to understand the physics of intracluster medium.

Radio Galaxies & Large-Scale Structures

Exploring radio galaxies, their morphology and evolution. Studying the connection between large-scale cosmic structures and the radio emission they produce, providing insights into the magnetized cosmic web.
